ScotlandPHP Review Board Application
Firstly we would like to outline a little of what will be involved and the process we follow before you decide to offer your assistance. Our review process is handled by a 5 step process. Initially we are looking for 3 additional reviewers (preferably people with some previous involvement in the PHP developer community in Scotland) to help us narrow down the huge number of submissions we have received. Step 1: Anonymous abstract review We use OpenCFP to handle submissions but have modified to hide all speaker information by default, leaving abstracts to be read for each submission. We are aware this isn't perfect as there are references in the abstracts/description which may indicate who the author is but feel it is still helpful. Each abstract is reviewed with a simple +1 / -1 system, with the idea of narrowing the pool to remove marketing/too heavily promotional/lower quality submissions as efficiently as possible. Step 2: Blind detailed review (only for talks that received +1 in step 1) Each talk is now reviewed again with comments left against with reviewers overall thoughts and a rating (between 1-3) for each of: * Quality of Submission * Relevance of Topic * Originality vs previous years selected talks Any talks that we decide should not be considered beyond this point are marked as -1 to avoid re-reviews and make management easier. Step 3: Sighted review (all talks passed through step 2) Review all talks again with visibility of speaker/slides/videos and additional information available in their bios. This is used to add an additional 1-3 score for speaker experience and appropriateness for conference. Step 4: Categorisation and Shortlisting All submissions are grouped by general categories of submission based around the content area covered. Within each of the given categories all members of the review board assign shortlists of talks they would be happy to have on the final schedule. Shortlists are shared openly between all reviewers and commonalities are pulled out into a master category shortlist. Step 5: Scheduling Combined shortlists are then discussed in group meetings (usually lasting hours) where we try to find the best balance of categories/speakers/talks/workshops to make the conference as beneficial to attendees as we can. What can you do? We will require people to be available to assist and feedback to the core conference team for 2 weeks starting the 18th April (next week). Initially we will require applicants to be able to commit to helping with the first 2 steps of our review process but can be brought in to the rest of the process if they would like/are able. Application process All applicants will be considered by the core conference team and we will reach out to successful candidates by the week beginning 15th of April. Once confirmed an onboarding meeting will be arranged between yourself and one of our existing core team to go through the process, set you up in our systems and make sure you are happy to start reviewing talks. We look forward to hearing from you! Any issues filling out this form or if you have any questions please contact us on organisers@scotlandphp.co.uk
